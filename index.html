Kitesurfing on Koh Phangan

Best kite spot in Thailand

Kite Club Koh Phangan sits directly on Thong Sala Beach — a reef-protected flat-water lagoon in the Gulf of Thailand. The northeast monsoon delivers 15–22 knot winds from January to April, with up to 90% rideable days in March. Sandy bottom, 28 °C water, shallow for 100+ metres — the safest teaching environment in Southeast Asia.

IKO & IWO certified school

All instructors hold IKO (International Kiteboarding Organization) and IWO certifications. Our multilingual team teaches in English, Russian, Arabic, German and Ukrainian. Established in 2021, we have trained hundreds of riders from complete beginner to independent level. Courses from 3,500 THB include all equipment and a personal progression plan.

Most beginners reach independent riding in 6–10 hours — typically 3–5 days. The Discovery Course (2h) covers kite control and body drag. The Full Beginner Course (6h/3 days) takes you to first water rides. The Independent Rider Course (10h/5 days) gets you riding upwind on your own with an IKO certificate. Progress depends on wind conditions and prior board sport experience.

Yes — Koh Phangan is one of the best places to learn in Southeast Asia. Thong Sala Beach has a reef-protected flat-water lagoon, sandy bottom, 15–25 knots of consistent NE trade wind from January to April, and 28°C water.
