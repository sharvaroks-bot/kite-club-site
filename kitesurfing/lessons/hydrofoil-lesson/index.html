Take flight above the water on foil

Setup and first water contact

  • Foil board anatomy: mast, fuselage, front wing and back wing explained on the beach
  • Foot placement: weight distribution front-to-back for stable flight
  • Body drag with the foil board — feel underwater drag and resistance
  • Waterstart on foil: board pop and immediate edge control

First flights

  • Lift trigger: the exact speed and kite angle at which the board rises
  • Height control: front-foot pressure to stay low and avoid a breach
  • Crash recovery: how to fall safely away from the foil mast
  • Riding upwind on foil: edge, lean and maintain altitude simultaneously

Conditions & logistics

  • 1h private session · flat-water lagoon · instructor supervision
  • Foil board equipment provided · IKO certified instructor
  • Prerequisite: Independent Rider level — must ride upwind confidently

FAQ

You must be able to ride independently upwind on a regular kiteboard before attempting hydrofoil. The foil adds complexity and requires good kite control and board skills.

Most students get their first sustained flights within the 1-hour session. Your instructor will set up the foil at an appropriate mast height to give you the best chance of early success.

Yes. All equipment is included — foil board, hydrofoil, kite, bar, harness, helmet and impact vest. You do not need to bring or own any foiling equipment.

Like all kitesurfing disciplines, hydrofoil has risks. We mitigate these with proper instruction, impact protection, correct foil settings and choosing appropriate conditions. Students with good independent riding skills progress safely.
