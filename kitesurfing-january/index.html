Why January is Perfect for Beginners

15–24 kts
Ideal Learning Wind
Winds arrive in January, building steadily as they shift from NE to SE/E by mid-month. 15–24 knots is the perfect range for beginners — enough power to ride, not too overpowering to control.
Quiet beach
Uncrowded Water
January sees far fewer kite tourists than February–April. The lagoon is spacious, instructors can give you more individual attention, and you progress faster with room to make mistakes.
Flat water
Glassy Lagoon
The NE wind blows cross-shore at Thong Sala, keeping the shallow lagoon flat and smooth. No chop, no swell — just clean water from day one of your lessons.

January Questions

January is one of the best months to learn kitesurfing on Koh Phangan. The SE/E trade winds open the season at 15–24 knots — consistent enough to ride, gentle enough for beginners to control. The beach is quieter than March–April, so instructors can give more individual attention. Lessons almost never get cancelled in January.

Yes — January is significantly quieter than February–April. Many kite tourists time their trip to March or April for peak wind, which means January offers a more relaxed experience with easier lesson bookings and more space on the water. If you want a premium learning environment without the peak-season rush, January is ideal.

January: 15–24 kts (SE season opening). February: 18–26 kts. March: 20–28 kts (peak). April: 18–26 kts (tapering). May–December: transition and SW season (15–22 kts at Baan Tai). January is the most beginner-friendly wind window of the SE season.

January is dry, warm, and sunny. Air temperature is 28–30°C, water temperature is 27°C. There is very little rainfall — January is one of the driest months of the year on the island. The trade wind blows across the lagoon keeping the air fresh. Ideal conditions for spending all day on the water.
