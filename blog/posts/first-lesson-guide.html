First Lesson Guide

What to Expect on Your
First Kite Lesson

Your first kitesurfing lesson at Kite Club Koh Phangan is 2 hours. Here is a step-by-step walkthrough of everything that happens — so you arrive relaxed and ready.

Before You Arrive

We confirm your lesson slot via WhatsApp the evening before and send the exact meeting point on Thong Sala Beach. Bring: swimwear you can move in, strong waterproof sunscreen (SPF 50+), a water bottle. Rash guard recommended — you will be in direct sun for 2 hours. Leave jewellery and watches at home.

On the Beach: Theory (30 minutes)

Your instructor starts with 20–30 minutes of theory on the beach: wind theory (the wind window — where the power is, where it is safe), kite anatomy (leading edge, struts, bridles), safety systems (quick-release bar, chicken loop, leash), and the IKO hand signals used when your instructor is not beside you.

This is not a boring lecture — it is the knowledge that keeps you safe and makes everything else easier to understand. Most students are surprised how logical it all is.

Trainer Kite on the Beach (30 minutes)

You start with a small trainer kite (2–3m²) to feel the wind window with minimal power. You will fly figure-of-8 patterns, practice parking the kite, and feel the difference between the power zone (12 o'clock) and the safe zone (edges). By the end of 30 minutes most students fly the trainer kite confidently with one hand.

Full Kite, Body Drag in the Water (60 minutes)

The second half of the lesson moves to the water with a full-size kite (6–9m², chosen for the wind conditions). Your instructor fits you with an impact vest and helmet. You enter the water and do your first body drag — using the kite to pull you through the water without a board. This builds kite steering muscle memory and is the foundation of the waterstart (next lesson).

Most students are surprised how powerful the kite feels in the water. The adrenaline is real. By the end of 2 hours you will have flown the kite in the water, completed a body drag, and have a clear sense of what comes next.

After the Lesson: What Comes Next?

After your Discovery Course your instructor will assess your level and recommend next steps. Most students proceed to the Full Beginner Course (3 × 2h sessions) to reach first rides on the board. There is no time pressure — you can book the next session the following day or return on a future trip and continue from your IKO level.
