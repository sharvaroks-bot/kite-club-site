Island Guide

10 Best Beaches
on Koh Phangan

Koh Phangan has more beaches than most visitors realise. This is the honest guide — which ones are worth the journey, and which are best skipped.

1. Thong Sala Beach — The Kite Spot

The long south-coast beach in front of Thong Sala town. Not the island's most scenic beach, but it punches well above its weight for function. Long stretch of flat sand, calm bay water, consistent NE wind from February to April. This is where Kite Club operates. Water condition: flat, waist-depth launch zone. Best for: kitesurfing, wing foiling, casual evening walks. Access: 5-minute walk from Thong Sala pier.

2. Haad Rin — The Famous One

On the southeastern tip of the island, Haad Rin is Koh Phangan's most internationally known beach — home of the Full Moon Party (once a month). Haad Rin Nok (east beach) is genuinely beautiful: powdery white sand, clear turquoise water, dramatic headland views. During the day and outside party weeks it is a pleasant beach. Water condition: some waves at low tide, can be rocky on the headland ends. Best for: swimming in good tidal conditions, people-watching, the Full Moon Party experience. Access: 30-minute songthaew from Thong Sala (negotiate price, usually 100–150 THB per person).

3. Bottle Beach (Haad Khuat) — Most Scenic

The most beautiful beach on the island by most measures — a perfect curved bay with turquoise water, fine white sand, jungle backdrop, and almost no development. The only catch: it is only accessible by longtail boat (approximately 100–150 THB per person from Haad Khom or Mae Haad) or a long hike through jungle. No road access keeps it pristine. Water condition: crystal clear, calm in NE season, some swell possible in SW season. Best for: day trip snorkelling and swimming, photography. Not for: kitesurfing. Note: there is minimal shade infrastructure and no ATMs — bring cash and sunscreen.

4. Haad Yao — West Coast Classic

Long sandy west-coast beach, about 15 minutes from Thong Sala by scooter. Good facilities — restaurants, beach bars, some accommodation. The west coast is sheltered from the NE trade wind, so it is calm and good for swimming in NE season. In SW season the wind picks up from the opposite direction, making it slightly choppier. Water condition: clear, gradual depth. Best for: swimming, beach restaurants, sunset watching. Access: scooter or taxi from Thong Sala, approximately 80–100 THB.

5. Haad Salad — Quiet with Good Snorkelling

Small, calm bay north of Haad Yao on the west coast. Less busy than Haad Yao, with better snorkelling — the rocky headland at the north end of the bay has coral and fish. Some decent budget accommodation nearby. Water condition: very calm, clear. Best for: snorkelling, quiet swimming, families. Access: scooter north from Haad Yao, or taxi from Thong Sala (around 30 minutes, 100–120 THB).

6. Haad Thong Nai Pan — Remote East Coast Gem

Two connected bays on the northeast coast — Thong Nai Pan Noi (smaller, more developed) and Thong Nai Pan Yai (larger, slightly wilder). Very scenic: jungle-backed, clear water, relatively uncrowded by Thai island standards. The road in is steep and rough in parts — takes about 40 minutes from Thong Sala by scooter or taxi. Worth the journey for a day trip or a few nights in a quieter setting. Water condition: usually calm, good swimming. Best for: those wanting quieter beach accommodation away from Thong Sala nightlife.

7. Haad Son (Secret Beach) — Dramatic and Secluded

Accessed via a rough track and a short walk through jungle, Haad Son rewards the effort with a dramatic small cove framed by large boulders. Very small beach, gets crowded if more than 20 people arrive. The rocks make it scenic but limit swimming area. Best visited on a weekday morning before tour groups arrive. Water condition: clear but wave exposure varies with season. Best for: photography, atmosphere, a different experience from the main beaches. Access: scooter only, and ask locally for the unmarked turnoff — it is easy to miss.

8. Mae Haad — The Sandbar Island

Mae Haad is on the northwest coast and is known for a distinctive sandbar that extends to a small island (Koh Ma) at low tide — you can walk across. Genuinely unique feature that photographs extremely well. The main beach is calm and flat. There is a small kite scene here in certain wind conditions, but the sandbar and swimmers make it less suitable than Thong Sala for lessons. Water condition: very calm, shallow sandbar. Best for: the sandbar walk, swimming, snorkelling around Koh Ma. Access: songthaew from Thong Sala, about 30–40 minutes.

9. Haad Chao Phao — Family-Friendly West Coast

Calm, wide beach on the west coast, good for families with children. Several beach bars and restaurant options. Sheltered from NE wind in peak season, so water is very calm. Less busy than Haad Yao but with similar quality. Water condition: flat and safe. Best for: families, relaxed swimming, beach dining. Access: 15 minutes south of Haad Yao on the west coast road.

10. Ao Plaay Laem — Closest to Thong Sala

Small bay just south of Thong Sala town, within walking or cycling distance. Not the island's most impressive beach, but perfectly adequate for an evening swim after a kite session. Local restaurants and small shops nearby. Useful if you are staying in Thong Sala and want a quick beach fix without travelling across the island. Water condition: calm, sheltered. Best for: convenience, evening swims for kite students based in town.
